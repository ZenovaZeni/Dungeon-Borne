# Polish Standard

## Definition Of Working

Prototype code existing is not enough.

A feature is working only when the player can understand and feel what happened.

For combat, this means:

- The input responds quickly.
- The action is visible.
- The hit is readable.
- The enemy reaction is noticeable.
- Damage feedback appears.
- Cooldown or availability is understandable.

## Combat Feel Standards

Movement must feel:

- Responsive
- Camera-relative
- Stable under mobile-style input
- Comfortable in an angled top-down view

Dash must have:

- Immediate movement
- Clear direction
- Visual feedback
- Audio feedback later
- Cooldown readability

Basic attack must have:

- Short front hit feedback
- Hit flash or enemy reaction
- Damage number
- Distinct feel from just walking into an enemy

Cleave must have:

- Wider visual footprint than basic attack
- Stronger impact read
- Clear forward direction
- Echo Axe mutation hook

Stomp must have:

- Clear AOE circle
- Obvious impact timing
- Stronger/heavier feel than Cleave

Rage placeholder must have:

- Clear activation
- Distinct visual language from normal attacks
- No full rage resource yet

## Enemy Standards

Enemy attacks must become readable before content expands.

Minimum standards:

- Skeleton Grunt chases and melee attacks.
- Archer keeps enough distance to feel ranged and shoots visible projectiles.
- Brute moves slower and hits harder.
- Attacks should telegraph before they become final.
- Enemy death should be clear, not just an instant disappearance.

## Feedback Standards

Prototype 0.1 should include temporary feedback even if ugly:

- Damage numbers
- Hit flash
- Attack markers
- Loot glow or label
- Shockwave visibility

Later polish should add:

- Animation
- VFX
- Sound
- Camera shake on heavy hits
- Better cooldown UI
- Enemy telegraphs

## Mobile Readability

Mobile readability matters more than visual detail.

Every important action should be understandable on a small screen:

- Player position
- Enemy position
- Enemy attack intent
- Skill area
- Damage
- Loot drops
- Cooldowns

If an effect looks cool but cannot be read on a phone, it is not good enough.
