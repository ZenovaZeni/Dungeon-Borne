# Visual Style Guide

## Style Target

Dungeon Borne should be stylized low-poly dark fantasy.

It should not look like:

- Realistic AAA fantasy
- A toy-like cartoon
- A Roblox-style block game
- A noisy asset-store collage

It should aim for:

- Strong silhouettes
- Dark dungeon spaces
- Colorful readable magic
- Clear enemy shapes
- Simple materials with strong lighting
- Mobile-first readability

## Visual Priorities

The first visual target is one polished dungeon room, not the whole game.

That room should eventually establish the quality bar for:

- Dungeon floor and wall kit
- Torch or magical lighting
- Fog and depth
- Shadowed walls
- Glowing loot
- Clear skill VFX
- Simple enemy models
- Better camera framing
- Readable UI
- Impactful sound
- One memorable weapon model, likely Echo Axe

## Low-Poly Rules

Models should be:

- Low-poly
- Readable at phone size
- Built around strong silhouettes
- Light on texture complexity
- Optimized for mobile

Avoid high-detail models that only look good up close.

## Color And Lighting

The dungeon can be dark, but gameplay cannot be unclear.

Use contrast intentionally:

- Enemies should stand out from floors and walls.
- Player skill VFX should use distinct colors.
- Loot should glow enough to notice.
- Enemy attack warnings should be brighter than ambient lighting.

Dark fantasy does not mean muddy visuals.

## Skill Visual Identity

Temporary Prototype 0.1 readability targets:

- Basic attack: short front flash or hitbox.
- Cleave: wider forward arc or heavy front marker.
- Stomp: clear circular area.
- Dash: blue streak, trail, or flash.
- Rage: red/gold glow or obvious activation marker.
- Echo Axe shockwave: visible traveling projectile.

Final VFX can replace the debug primitives later.

## Asset Direction

Use simple placeholder primitives until the mechanic works.

Once mechanics are proven, prefer assets that support:

- Low-poly dungeon kits
- Stylized heroes and enemies
- Readable weapons
- Simple magic VFX
- Mobile RPG UI icons
- Fantasy combat audio

Do not let asset packs redefine the architecture.
