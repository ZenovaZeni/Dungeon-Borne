# World, Story, and Chapters

## Story Direction

The story needs a better reason than "adventurers go into a dungeon to die."

Stronger premise:

A town exists because of the dungeon, not in spite of it.

Generations ago, the dungeon produced relics, healing stones, rare metals, and class sigils that allowed the town to become wealthy. Families, guilds, and orders built their lives around controlled expeditions.

Then the dungeon changed.

The deeper floors sealed. The old orders vanished. Monsters began wearing the armor of dead heroes. Relics started corrupting people. Now the town is declining, divided, and desperate.

The player is not going down just for treasure.

The player goes down because:
- someone important vanished
- the town's protective relic is failing
- the dungeon is spreading upward
- old class orders left secrets below
- the player's bloodline is tied to the dungeon

## Tone

Dark fantasy, but not miserable.

Low-poly stylized with colorful magic.

Think:
- mysterious town
- ancient class orders
- cursed relics
- dungeon mystery
- bosses with story
- simple emotional motivation

## Chapter Structure

### Chapter 1: The Hollow Below
Town: Ashvale
Dungeon: old mine/crypt beneath the town
Theme: forgotten heroes and corrupted relics

### Chapter 2: Icebound Keep
Town/Outpost: Frostmere
Dungeon: frozen castle
Theme: oathbreakers, knights, frost magic

### Chapter 3: Dragon Furnace
Town/Outpost: Emberfall
Dungeon: lava forge/ancient dragon engine
Theme: blacksmiths, dragons, fire relics

## Cutscene Types

### In-Engine Cutscenes
Use Unity timeline/cinemachine style camera work:
- gate opens
- boss intro
- relic activation
- town event

### Motion Comic Panels
Use still images, text, music, and voiceover.
Good for chapter intros and trailers.

### Dialogue Scenes
NPC portrait or simple in-world camera shot with text.

## Story Rule

Story should support the dungeon loop.

Do not write a novel before the prototype is fun.
